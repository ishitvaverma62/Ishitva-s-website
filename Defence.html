Introduction

- India is among the top 12 destinations for defence worldwide and 4th largest military spending nations in the world. - In 2020-21, India’s defence budget allocation increased by 3 per cent, or Rs. 3,400 crore and will focus on acquisitions for and modernisation of the three services. - India has been pushing for greater indigenisation of defence hardware as India imports around 70 percent (by value) of its high-tech defence equipment mainly from Russia, Japan, Israel and the United States. - India has signed defence cooperation agreements and MoUs with over 20 countries including United States (as a ‘major defence partner),’ enabling license-free access to a wide range of dual-use technologies.

Challenges

- The complex and heavily regulated environment poses significant obstacles, as navigating the intricate maze of guidelines and approvals from multiple regulatory bodies can be time-consuming and resource-intensive. - Budget constraints, especially for smaller players, hinder their ability to invest in advanced technologies and research and development. - Technological gaps, especially in areas such as cyber security, artificial intelligence, and space, limit India’s defence capabilities and create dependence on foreign suppliers. - Security threats, such as cross-border terrorism, cyber attacks, and maritime disputes, require constant vigilance and preparedness from the defence forces.
