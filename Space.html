Space (Chronological Order)

Here is some data about the 'space' revolution in India according to the timeline: - 1962: The Indian National Committee for Space Research (INCOSPAR) was established by Prime Minister Jawaharlal Nehru and scientist Vikram Sarabhai to coordinate space research in India. - 1963: India launched its first rocket, the Nike-Apache, from Thumba Equatorial Rocket Launching Station (TERLS) near Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala. - 1969: The Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) was formed as the successor of INCOSPAR. - 1975: India launched its first satellite, Aryabhata, using a Soviet rocket. - 1980: India became the sixth country to launch a satellite, Rohini RS-1, using its own rocket, the Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV). - 1984: India sent its first astronaut, Rakesh Sharma, to space aboard the Soviet Soyuz T-11 mission. - 1992: India launched its first remote sensing satellite, IRS-1B, using its Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV). - 1999: India launched its first geostationary satellite, INSAT-2E, using its Geosynchronous Satellite Launch Vehicle (GSLV). - 2008: India launched its first lunar mission, Chandrayaan-1, using a PSLV. The mission discovered the presence of water molecules on the moon. - 2013: India launched its first interplanetary mission, Mars Orbiter Mission (MOM), or Mangalyaan, using a PSLV. India became the fourth country and the first Asian nation to reach Mars orbit, and the first to do so on its maiden attempt. - 2016: India launched a record 20 satellites in a single PSLV mission, including its own Cartosat-2 series satellite and 19 foreign satellites. - 2017: India launched a record 104 satellites in a single PSLV mission, including its own Cartosat-2 series satellite and 103 foreign satellites. - 2019: India launched its second lunar mission, Chandrayaan-2, using a GSLV Mk III. The mission attempted to land a rover on the moon's south pole, but lost contact with the lander during the final descent. - 2021: India launched its first manned space mission, Gaganyaan, using a GSLV Mk III. The mission carried three Indian astronauts to low Earth orbit for seven days. - 2022: India launched its first solar mission, Aditya-L1, using a PSLV. The mission studied the sun's corona and its influence on the Earth's climate
