BioTechnical Sector (Introduction)

- India is among the top 12 destinations for biotechnology worldwide and 3rd largest destination for biotechnology in Asia Pacific. - In 2023, India’s Biotechnology industry has crossed an estimated $92 Bn, growing 15% from the previous year. - India’s Biotechnology sector is categorised into Biopharmaceuticals, Bio agriculture, Bio IT and Bio Services - India is one of the biggest suppliers of low cost drugs and vaccines in the world.

BioTechnical Sector (Challenges)

- The complex and heavily regulated environment poses significant obstacles, as navigating the intricate maze of guidelines and approvals from multiple regulatory bodies can be time-consuming and resource-intensive. - Budget constraints, especially for smaller players, hinder their ability to invest in advanced technologies. - Resistance to change and apprehension about disrupting established processes further hinder innovation within the sector.

Trends and Predictionss

- AI's pervasive influence in healthcare will expand, affecting drug development, disease diagnosis, treatment planning, and patient care management. - The IoMT will experience exponential growth, connecting medical devices through wireless networks, enabling remote monitoring of vital signs, and empowering patients to take control of their health. - The relentless growth in data generated by healthcare organizations will drive the increased use of big data analytics. - India is poised as one of the leading destinations for bioinnovation and biomanufacturing, and hence is identified as a sunrise sector and a key part of India’s vision of reaching a $5 Tn Economy by 2024.
